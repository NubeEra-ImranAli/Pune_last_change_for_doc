Histogram of Pollutant Concentrations

A histogram can be used to visualize the distribution of a pollutant (e.g., PM2.5, PM10, NO2, etc.) across all locations. It helps understand the frequency of different concentration ranges.

Box Plot for Pollutant Concentrations

A box plot can show the distribution of pollutants, highlighting the median, quartiles, and any outliers.

Scatter Plot for Correlation between Two Pollutants

Scatter plots can visualize the relationship between two pollutants, such as PM2.5 vs PM10, NO2 vs CO, etc. This is helpful for identifying any linear or non-linear correlations.

Stacked Bar Chart for Multiple Pollutants

A stacked bar chart can compare multiple pollutants across locations to see how they contribute to air quality at each location.

Area Plot for Multiple Pollutants

An area plot can show the contribution of each pollutant over time or locations.

Radar Chart for Pollutant Levels Across Locations

Radar charts are useful when comparing the levels of multiple pollutants across different locations. Each axis represents a different pollutant.
